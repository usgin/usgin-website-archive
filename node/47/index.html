From Geodatabase to ArcMap, Part 2

Your data will appear in the Layers panel on the left side of the screen. In order to display the data points within this data, you must first tell ArcMap what projection to use for your data, and which fields within your dataset contain the latitude and longitude coordinates.

  1. Right-click on your data. In the menu that apears (Figure 13), click Display XY Data

    Figure 13:Displaying XY Data

  2. In the Display XY Data window that appears (Figure 14), make sure that your X and Y fields correspond with the database fields in your database that contain the latitude and longitude information for your data. Warning: errors sometimes occur, so it’s possible you may have the right data in the wrong field. If your data does not display correctly, don’t be afraid to experiment with the X Field and Y Field options in order to find the right combination.

    Figure 14: XY Data

  3. After you have specified your X Field and your Y Field, click Edit to specify the coordinate system [Dead Link, http://help.arcgis.com/en/arcgisdesktop/10.0/help/index.html/content/glossary#//003r00000001000000.htm] for your data. Your dataset will specify the correct coordinate system under the UTMDatumZone column. If your data set does not so specify, the most common coordinate systems for North American data are NAD27 (North American Datum, 1927) NAD83 (North American Datum 1983), and WGS84; best practice for SGD uses the WGS84 (World Geodetic System 1984). If your data corresponds with features in North America and no coordinate system is provided under the UTMDatumZone field in your data set, it should be safe to use one of the three; some degree of trial-and-error could be necessary.

    Figure 15: Select your coordinate system

  4. Clicking Edit will bring the Spatial Reference Properties window (Figure 15). Click Select to manually choose a coordinate system for your data. Clicking Select will bring up a window in which you can choose from two folders (Figure 16): Geographic Coordinate Systems and Projected Coordinate Systems. Select Geographic Coordinate Systems and click Add.

    Figure 16: Browsing for coordinate systems

  5. Selecting Geographic Coordinate Systems and clicking Add in the Browse for Coordinate System window (Figure 16) will bring you to a directory in which you must choose the country of origin of your coordinate system. If you are using the NAD 27 or NAD 83 coordinate system, the country of origin of these coordinate systems would be North America. If you are using the WGS84 coordinate system, the source would be World Select the folder that corresponds with the country of origin for your coordinate system and click Add.

    Figure 17: Coordinate systems

  6. Selecting a coordinate system and click Add in the Browse for Coordinate Systems window depicted in Figure 17 will bring you back to the Spatial Reference Properties window in Figure 15, but the appearance of the window will have changed to reflect the coordinate system you selected (Figure 18). Click OK to continue.

    Figure 18: The Spatial Reference Properties window with a coordinate system selected

  7. Clicking OK in the Spatial Reference Properties window in Figure 18 will bring you to the Display XY Data window in Figure 14, but this window will likewise be changed to reflect your newly selected coordinate system (Figure 19)

    Figure 19: Displaying XY data with a coordinate system selected

  8. Click OK. With luck, your data should now display in the Map window of ArcMap (Figure 20). This is how your data will look when it goes live as a service.

    Figure 20: Coordinates displaying properly

  9. Before you can save your map, you need to adjust the properties of your newly created data. Right-click on your data and click Properties (Figure 21).

    Figure 21: Layer properties

  10. Clicking Properties will bring up the Layer Properties window (Figure 22). Under the General tab, provide the basic information about your dataset, including a description and credits. Under the Symbology tab, you will need to alter the legend to coincide with SGD standards. When finished, click OK.

    Figure 22: The Layer Properties window

  11. Under the File menu, at the top of the screen, click Map Document Properties (Figure 23). Fill out all summary information; also, make sure you set the Default Geodatabase to the one containing your data, and check Store Relative Pathnames to Data Sources.

    Figure 23: The Map Properties window

  12. Under the File menu at the top of the screen, click Save, and then specify a location on your computer. Best practice is to save your map as a .mxd file in proximity to your geodatabase. For example, if your geodatabase was located under
    C:\Data\Borehole_Data\CA_Borehole_Data\Data

    then best practice would be to either save your .mxd file in the same folder, or in

    C:\Data\Borehole_Data\CA_Borehole_Data\Project

Remember: the appearance of your map file determines the appearance of your data when it goes live. What you see is what you get.
