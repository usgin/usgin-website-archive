Management and history

A partnership of the Association of American State Geologists (AASG - state geological surveys) and the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) formally agreed in 2007 to develop a national geoscience information framework that is distributed, interoperable, uses open source standards and common protocols, respects and acknowledges data ownership, fosters communities of practice to grow, and develops new web services and clients. The AASG and USGS have formed an interagency Steering Committee to pursue design and implementation of the Geoscience Information Network (GIN).


In Fall, 2008,  a coalition of the state geological surveys, the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS), GEON, and other partners received about $625,000 from NSF under the INTEROP solicitation to start building a distributed, interoperable data network. AASG and USGS estimate they collectively maintain over 3,000 data bases or date sets.  The NSF project will develop standardized services to make these data accessible online in a distributed network using a few standards and protocols, and work with data providers to implement these services. This project The Geosciences Information Network project will be managed by the Arizona Geological Survey on behalf of the Association of American State Geologists (AASG) in partnership with the USGS.

The kick-off meeting for a 5-year project to develop a National Geothermal Data System (NGDS)was held in Boise, Idaho Nov. 2 and 3, 2009. This work is supported by an award of $5 million from US Dept. of Energy to a coalition lead by Walt Synder at Boise State University. The Arizona Geological Survey is a partner in the NGDS coalition, acting on behalf of the Association of American State Geologists, and will lead the work to develop system integration, data access and discovery, and ensure interoperability of the NGDS as part of the burgeoning national GeoScience Information Network.   Other coalition organizations are providing data content and desktop applications using GIN service profiles.

 On October 30, 2009, Energy Secretary Stephen Chu announced that a coalition of 40 state geological surveys lead by the Arizona Geological Survey acting on behalf of the Association of American State Geologists (AASG - www.stategeologists.org) has been selected to develop content for the National Geothermal Data System with relevant state-specific geothermal data integrated using Geoscience Information Network services. The project is currently in negotiation to receive $17.7 million over 3 years to support subcontracted geothermal data compilation and publication in all 50 states using interoperable web services.
